Core Principles of Strategic L&D

These principles emerge directly from enterprise-level practice — not theory alone.

PRINCIPLE 01
Align to Business Strategy First

Every learning initiative must trace back to a business priority. At Amazon, building a COE meant aligning with 9 regulatory stakeholders before a single course was built. Strategy precedes design.

PRINCIPLE 02
Governance Enables Scale

Without intake governance and roadmap frameworks, programs stall at department level. Structured charters, intake criteria, and prioritization models transform L&D from reactive to strategic.

PRINCIPLE 03
Measure Impact, Not Just Completion

Completion rates are a vanity metric. Meaningful measurement ties learning to performance outcomes, engagement scores, and business KPIs — as demonstrated by NPS tracking at Altria.

PRINCIPLE 04
Lead with Stakeholder Trust

Senior stakeholder partnership isn't optional — it's the foundation. L&D leaders who build trust with business unit heads unlock funding, access, and cultural adoption that cannot be mandated from below.

Leading Across Cultures

Five principles for managing global learning design teams with empathy and effectiveness.

PRINCIPLE 01
Culture First, Content Second

Before designing a single slide, understand how learners in each region receive information. Hierarchical cultures (China, India) respond differently to instruction than egalitarian ones (Poland, UK). Design must match the cultural context, not override it.

PRINCIPLE 02
Language is More Than Translation

AI translation handles words — but human cultural experts handle meaning. The Dalian and Iași teams don't just translate content; they transform it. A concept that lands perfectly in English may require full reframing to resonate in Mandarin or Romanian.

PRINCIPLE 03
Time Zones as a Feature, Not a Bug

A team spanning Florida, London, Poland, Romania, India, and China creates a 24-hour design capability. With the right handoff protocols and governance, global distribution accelerates production — it doesn't slow it down.

PRINCIPLE 04
Psychological Safety Transcends Borders

Feedback norms vary enormously by culture. Building a team culture where a designer in Gdańsk and a designer in Bangalore both feel equally safe challenging a design decision requires intentional, ongoing leadership effort.

PRINCIPLE 05
Celebrate the Differences That Improve the Work

The best global learning programs aren't culturally neutral — they're culturally informed. When your Pune team flags that a scenario feels too Western, that's not a problem to fix. That's the quality control working exactly as intended.

Cultural Insights That Improve Learning

What each regional team teaches us about designing better global learning.

🇨🇳

Bangalore and Pune teams flagged early that desktop-first eLearning design fails in India's primarily mobile workforce. Their advocacy led to a mobile-responsive redesign that expanded reach to millions of frontline learners previously locked out.

How I Work · Design Process

From Business Problem to Measurable Outcome

This is my actual process — not a generic ADDIE waterfall. It's iterative, stakeholder-anchored, and measurement-first. Every step maps to a CPTD capability area.

1
CPTD · Consulting & Business Partnering
Diagnose Before Designing

I don't accept a course request at face value. I start with a performance problem — who can't do what, under what conditions, and what's the cost to the business? I use structured needs analysis to separate training solutions from process, resource, or motivation gaps. Many "training requests" are process problems in disguise.

Real evidence: At Amazon, aligning 9 regulatory stakeholders before building the COE was the diagnostic phase — understanding the real governance gap before designing a single programme.
2
CPTD · Evaluating Learning Impact
Define Success Before You Design

Before a single slide or module is designed, I establish what Level 3 (behaviour change) and Level 4 (business results) look like. NPS, CSAT, error reduction rates, time-to-productivity metrics — these are determined in partnership with business stakeholders at the start, not measured as an afterthought at the end.

Real evidence: TrackWise — the 34% entry error reduction and 93% CSAT were designed-in metrics, not post-hoc discoveries. They were the goal before development began.
3
CPTD · Instructional Design · Learning Sciences
Design for Transfer, Not Just Completion

I apply cognitive load theory, spaced practice, and retrieval-based learning to design experiences that transfer to the job — not just score well on a post-test. Formats are chosen based on what the behaviour requires: performance support for process tasks, scenario-based learning for judgement calls, facilitated labs for leadership and culture.

Real evidence: The Altria intern StrengthsFinder programme — 600 reports analysed, 10 two-hour labs in 4 days — was designed around social learning and immediate application, not a deck and a quiz.
4
CPTD · Cultural Awareness & Inclusion · Training Delivery
Facilitate for the Actual Audience

Facilitation is not presenting. I design learning experiences where participants do the work — solve problems, apply models, give each other feedback. I adapt delivery to cultural context: what drives psychological safety in Bangalore is different from Gdańsk or Dalian. I design for those differences, not around them.

AI Translation at Scale · STAR Case Study
$500,000 Saved on One Safety Module — Then Replicated Across the Enterprise
Situation
Amazon's global compliance library required translation into 26+ languages for 1.4M associates. The traditional vendor model cost $500K+ per module and took 6 weeks per language — making compliance delivery at scale financially unsustainable and chronically behind schedule.
Task
As the COE lead, I was tasked with finding a translation and localisation model that could keep pace with a 653-deliverable roadmap, meet regulatory timelines, and dramatically reduce cost — without sacrificing quality or learner experience across culturally diverse markets.
Action
I piloted and benchmarked an AI-first translation pipeline — selecting, validating, and building governance around AI tools against the existing vendor standard. I designed a hybrid quality assurance model: AI generates the translation, certified language reviewers close the gap to 100%. I built the workflow, trained the team, and scaled it across the full compliance library.
Result
AI translation scored 94.7% quality out-of-the-box — requiring minimal human review. The first module alone saved $500K vs. the vendor quote. Scaled across the pipeline: $5M+/yr in projected savings. Compliance modules now reach 1.4M associates in 26+ languages on schedule. The model became the enterprise standard for global learning design.
